# Programme Ideas

## Visits and Visitors

### [Code Club](https://codeclub.org/en/)
Get a visit from some experts to learn how to do some basic coding.

### [Commonwealth War Graves Commission](https://www.cwgc.org/our-work/outreach/speakers-programme/cwgc-talks/)
Book a speaker from the CWGC to learn about the history and remembrance of commonwealth war graves.

### [Go Outdoors Kit Talks](https://www.scouts.org.uk/supporters/go-outdoors/)
Probably more useful to older sections, to help them get to grips with the essentials for expeditions and nights away.

### [Guide Dogs](https://www.guidedogs.org.uk/how-you-can-help/invite-a-speaker/invite-a-speaker-form/)
Invite a speaker to talk about how guide dogs support visually impaired individuals and see a demonstration.

### [Hearing Dogs for Deaf People](https://www.hearingdogs.org.uk/how-you-can-help/fundraising/talks)
Learn about the vital training hearing dogs undergo to support deaf people and alert them to crucial sounds.

### [Met Office](https://www.metoffice.gov.uk/about-us/who-we-are/our-values/social-responsibility-and-community/schools-and-colleges)
Educational resources, talks, and insights into weather forecasting, meteorology, and climate change.

### [National Trust](https://www.nationaltrust.org.uk/support-us/volunteer/talks-service)
Arrange a volunteer speaker to share stories about local nature reserves, historic houses, and conservation work.

### [NSPCC - Speak Out, Stay Safe](https://learning.nspcc.org.uk/services/speak-out-stay-safe)
An age-appropriate programme helping children understand how to stay safe from abuse and who to turn to for help.

### [Pets at Home](https://community.petsathome.com/events/the-scouting-association/)
In-store workshops and partner events tailored to help young people learn about animal welfare and care.

### [UK Parliament](https://learning.parliament.uk/en/communities-and-youth-groups/)
Interactive sessions and speakers designed to teach youth groups about democracy, voting, and how parliament works.

## Local Ideas
You'll need to do some searching to get in touch with these organisations in your local area.

### Ambulance Station
Visit a local station or invite paramedics to learn essential first aid and explore an emergency vehicle.

### Banks
[HSBC](https://www.scouts.org.uk/supporters/hsbc-uk/) and [Metro Bank](https://www.metrobankonline.co.uk/about-us/kids-zone/) have offered this in the past — a great way to work towards the Money Skills badges.

### Fire Service
A staple trip for younger sections to learn about fire safety, emergency response, and see the station equipment.

### Police Station
Learn about community safety, law enforcement, and get an inside look at how a local station operates.

### Hackspace / Makerspace
These often have a huge variety of tools and equipment, as well as experts to show you how to use them and have fun. Think things like 3D printing, laser cutting, woodwork, and robots!

### Nature Reserves
Coordinate with local wardens for a guided night hike, bug hunting, pond dipping, or conservation work.

### RNLI / Coastguard
Essential for Sea Scouts or any group looking to cover water safety, sea rescues, and maritime emergency response.

### Search and Rescue
Invite a local mountain or lowland rescue team to demonstrate navigation, casualty evacuation, and tracking skills.

### Sports Clubs
Judo, Karate, Rugby, Lawn Bowls — anything you can think of to try a new sport or fulfill physical activity badges.

### Universities
Some universities have specialized facilities like mock-court rooms which make for excellent mock trials for Explorers.

## Other Places for Ideas

### [Online Scout Manager (OSM)](https://www.onlinescoutmanager.co.uk/main.php?j=p60385)
Only available to leaders with an account. You'll find a huge list under Programme > Activities. Don't forget to submit your own great ideas!

### [The Scouts' Planning Ideas Calendar](https://www.scouts.org.uk/volunteers/running-your-section/planning-your-programme/programme-planning-ideas-calendars/)
A seasonal calendar packed with key dates, international events, and structured programme inspiration.

### [The Scouts' List of Activity Ideas](https://www.scouts.org.uk/activities/)
The official database filtering hundreds of activities by section, time limit, and badge requirements.

### [The Scouts' Nights Away Activity Ideas](https://www.scouts.org.uk/volunteers/running-your-section/planning-your-programme/nights-away-and-camping/)
Specific guidance, games, and skills tailored to camping, bivouacking, and outdoor residentials.

### [The Scouts' Programme Planner](https://www.scouts.org.uk/programme-planner)
The automated digital tool that helps layout your term plans and cleanly link your evenings to badge criteria.
